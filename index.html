About

I am a researcher in computational biology and an assistant professor with a PhD in Computer Science & Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology Patna (2019–2023). My research focuses on multi-modal deep learning methods for cancer prognosis and medical image classification. I develop architectures that combine multi-omics, clinical, and histopathology data to predict cancer survival and build robust, interpretable models for real-world biomedical applications.

Research Interests & Projects

Interests: Computational Biology, Multi-modal Deep Learning, Cancer Prognosis, Histopathology & Dermoscopy Image Analysis, Generative Models for Missing Data, Graph Neural Networks, Fuzzy Ensembles.

Selected Projects

  • Multi-modal Pan-Cancer Survival Estimation — deep architectures using six modalities from TCGA PAN Cancer data for robust survival prediction.
  • Skin Lesion Classification — ensemble of transfer-learning based EfficientNets and fuzzy ensemble strategies to classify dermoscopy images into multiple classes.
  • Generative Incomplete Multi-View Prognosis Predictor (GIMPP) — generative models to impute missing modalities for prognosis prediction.
  • Graph-based Prognosis Models — Graph Convolutional Networks and contrastive learning for breast cancer survival prediction.

Short Research Statement

My research builds robust multi-modal deep learning systems that integrate histopathology, clinical and omics data to provide accurate, explainable prognosis predictions. I focus on methods that handle missing modalities, improve stability under real-world variability, and provide clinically meaningful explanations. I seek collaborations with clinical teams and interdisciplinary groups to translate ML models into usable decision-support tools.
